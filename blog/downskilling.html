Down skilling

Now this is a bit of a spicy one.

There's a phase being thrown around these days and that's to do with 'down skilling'. Not 'up skilling'.

And the reason is because AI is coming for all the 'behind the desk jobs'. Back in the day you would have been told that the best way to get ahead in life is to study as much as you can. However, now in regards to the AI, the precious commodity of intelligence is becoming readily available to everyone. Of course, it isn't perfect, but it is coming.

So the natural question that arises is what should you do? The answer might just be 'downskilling'. Basically, should you down skill to get a better job? My answer is - perhaps.

Make no mistake, if you can leverage your current skills to pull away, maybe you should consider this - whilst it is still profitable. But in the long run, I think you should be looking for other options that is difficult for AI to take over. Ironically, these type of jobs are traditionally the ones you would look down on. Is it worth 'downskilling' to keep your financial stability in the long run? Maybe, it is defintely something to consider.
