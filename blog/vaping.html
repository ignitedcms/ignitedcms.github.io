Vaping ban

Well disposable ones at least.

The UK has set a new precedent in regards to the law concerning disposable vapes. I happen to think it is definitely a step in the right direction. Don't get me wrong, sometimes I vape from time to time.

But the disposable vape market is so destructive, in regards to targeting youngsters and being terrible for the environment. I read somewhere that something in the order of 5 million disposable vaping products are binned each week.

At present, because vaping is fairly new, we don't actually know what harmful effects they have. In addition to this new ruling we can expect higher taxes.

And then, on top of this there are these even newer products - 'snus' or vape pouches which you place between the gums and upper lip. Often claimed to be even more addictive than regular vaping products.

Cut a long story short, if you're considering quitting, now would be a good time to start. There's plenty of products available, such as nicorette patches and gum. Then it is just a matter of lowering the strengths of these quitting products periodically.
