Finding Joy again

Let's be real - AI is kinda everywhere these days. Midjourney's making art, ChatGPT's cranking out code, and algorithms are dropping beats. So where does that leave us humans who love to create?


I mean, I've dedicated a large subset of my life to 'creating', be it stuff in blender, art for print, websites, and lately making all kinds of music in my DAW. But lately, I've kinda been wondering, what's the point. AI is eventually going to do a better job. This feels kinda rough.


Here's the thing: AI might be good at making stuff, but it doesn't feel anything when it creates. That's our superpower. When you're painting or coding or whatever, you're putting your whole messy, beautiful life experience into it. That's something no algorithm can touch.

Instead of trying to out-robot the robots:


Trust your weird, unique perspective. Your life experiences and how you see the world? Totally irreplaceable.


Enjoy the creative ride. Half the fun is in the process - those late-night breakthroughs and happy accidents that happen along the way.


Try making AI your sidekick instead of your replacement. Let it handle the boring stuff while you focus on the parts that need your human touch.


Hang with other creators. Nothing beats sharing ideas with people who get what you're going through.

Create with purpose. Ask yourself what you really wanna say with your work - something that's genuinely you.


Maybe it's not about humans vs. AI. Maybe it's about finding that sweet spot where tech handles the grunt work and we bring the soul.


At the end of the day, creativity isn't just about making stuff - it's about expressing yourself and connecting with others. And that's something AI just can't fake, no matter how good it gets.
