Robotics

Is there a future in robotics?

This is the next question I ask myself. Let's suppose we achieve artificial general intelligence. The next vector, after we've surpassed the AGI epoch, is seemingly trying to put this inside a robot, so it can actually interact with the world.

This isn't anything new. Tesla and others are already doing just this. But it begs the question. We're gonna need a lot of robots. And somebody needs to manufacture them.

So I guess, is there a future in the manufacturing of high precision engineered parts? Of course, I'm assuming we're gonna need parts made from metal. Maybe that's not necessarily true. Perhaps there is some plastic composite that can be 3D printed to roughly do a similar thing. Seems unlikely.

But assuming there is not. This leads me to believe being involved in this type of industry might be, at the very least, a reliable short term option.

Only time will tell. . .
